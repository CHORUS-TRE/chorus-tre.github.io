# About CHORUS

<Accordion summaryStyle={{ fontSize: '1.25rem', fontWeight: 'bold' }}>

<AccordionSection title="What is CHORUS?">

CHORUS is a secure, open-source Trusted Research Environment (TRE) designed for biomedical research and AI development. It provides a single, governed platform where sensitive health data can be accessed, analyzed, and shared safely, in full alignment with privacy, security, and regulatory requirements.

The platform brings together what is usually fragmented across multiple systems: secure workspaces, data governance workflows, privacy controls, and scientific tools — all in one place. From data access to analysis and result export, every step takes place inside a controlled, monitored, and auditable environment, reducing operational friction while increasing trust and compliance.

Learn more about CHORUS and access the demo on the <a href="https://chorus-tre.org" style={{ color: 'var(--chorus-blue)' }}>CHORUS website</a>.

</AccordionSection>

<AccordionSection title="Who is it for?">

CHORUS is built around the full research lifecycle, spanning study design, computation, governance, publication, and the ongoing reuse of data and knowledge.
Clinical researchers use it to design and run trials securely, data scientists build AI models with reproducible datasets, statisticians perform analyses with trusted tools like R or Jupyter, and data protection officers oversee compliance in real time.

Institutions benefit as well: hospitals, universities, and research consortia can deploy CHORUS to standardize governance, reduce compliance risk, and enable collaboration without exposing sensitive data.

</AccordionSection>

<AccordionSection title="What does it do?">

At its core, CHORUS enables secure research without friction.
It provides isolated, browser-accessible sessions where teams can analyze clinical data, train AI models, and generate reproducible results. Its built-in governance ensures that every dataset, tool, and user interaction is logged, controlled, and compliant by design.

Researchers can perform anything from cohort discovery and statistical analysis to imaging analysis, anonymization, and report generation — all within a governed, cloud-native environment.
Every operation takes place inside a defined "Safe" zone, implementing the Five Safes Framework (Safe People, Safe Projects, Safe Data, Safe Settings, Safe Outputs) directly in its architecture.

For step-by-step guidance on using CHORUS, visit the <a href="/docs/user-guide" style={{ color: 'var(--chorus-blue)' }}>User Guide</a>.

</AccordionSection>

<AccordionSection title="How is it built?">

Behind its simple interface lies a sophisticated, cloud-native foundation. CHORUS runs on Kubernetes, orchestrated through GitOps for traceability and reproducibility. It integrates single sign-on (SSO) via Keycloak, enforces multi-factor authentication for all users, and isolates every project within its own secure virtual workspace.

Data is stored using a hybrid architecture — object storage for long-term retention, high-performance tiers for computation — all fully encrypted. Monitoring, auditing, and compliance verification are automated. The result is an environment that's both highly secure and researcher-friendly, with minimal operational friction.

Dive into the architecture and governance design in the <a href="/docs/tech/technical-documentation" style={{ color: 'var(--chorus-blue)' }}>Technical Documentation</a>.

</AccordionSection>

<AccordionSection title="Why does it matter?">

Biomedical research is increasingly shaped by data-intensive methods, fast-moving technologies, and growing regulatory requirements. Data, compute resources, analytical tools, and governance are often scattered across disconnected systems, slowing down projects and limiting collaboration. At the same time, AI is becoming central to discovery, demanding scalable compute, high-quality data, and reproducible environments.

CHORUS brings everything needed for modern biomedical and AI-driven research into a single, governed platform: secure data access, high-performance computing, advanced analytical tools, and built-in regulatory compliance. By unifying infrastructure, workflows, and governance, it reduces friction, accelerates research cycles, and enables AI development at scale, while remaining aligned with regulatory requirements. The result is a platform that evolves with science and technology—and allows research to move faster without compromising trust.

Explore CHORUS’s compliance framework in <a href="/docs/security-privacy" style={{ color: 'var(--chorus-blue)' }}>Security & Privacy</a>.

</AccordionSection>

<AccordionSection title="How did it begin?">

<p style={{ fontSize: '0.95rem', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  CHORUS emerged as a response to a concrete clinical and scientific need: to securely share and analyze rare, high-value data across institutions. Its roots lie in the Human Intracerebral EEG Platform (HIP), a pioneering Trusted Research Environment (TRE) for neuroscience. What began as a focused solution for epilepsy research has since evolved into a secure, open-source platform for biomedical research and AI across disciplines.

<AccordionSection title="Where is it going?">

CHORUS is currently in active development and live across build, development, and QA environments. Its roadmap focuses on public beta releases, institutional pilot programs, and expansion across Swiss and European academic infrastructures.
Future development will enhance the app ecosystem, integrate new compliance automation features, and strengthen multi-cloud support through providers like Exoscale, CSCS, and SwitchCloud.

The long-term goal is not just to build software — it's to establish CHORUS as a European benchmark for open, secure, and sustainable research infrastructure.

Track milestones and releases in <a href="/docs/tech/release-notes" style={{ color: 'var(--chorus-blue)' }}>Release Notes</a>.
